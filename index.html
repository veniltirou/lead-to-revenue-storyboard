Chapter 1

Oracle CX Lead-to-Revenue Overview

The complete 15-scene end-to-end storyboard — from the very first marketing campaign all the way through to subscription renewal — showing how all Oracle CX products connect across a single B2B enterprise deal.

Business Scenario: ABC Corporation is a specialty vending solutions provider with Oracle CX fully implemented across their business. XYZ Corporation is a networking company that needs vending solutions for their offices. This storyboard follows the complete journey as ABC Corporation identifies, pursues, closes, fulfills and renews the deal with XYZ Corporation — using Oracle CX every step of the way.

The Full Lead-to-Revenue Flow

Oracle CX Products Featured in This Chapter

Product Role in the Journey
Oracle CX Sales Lead capture, qualification and opportunity management
Oracle CPQ Configure, price, quote and approval
Oracle Order Management Order orchestration and sequencing
Oracle Field Service Field technician dispatch and installation
Oracle Subscription Management Subscription activation and management
Oracle Fusion Service Post-sale service and entitlement management
Oracle Accounts Receivable Billing schedule and invoicing
Oracle Revenue Management (RMCS) Revenue recognition and reporting
Renewal Management Renewal preparation, upsell and cross-sell

Scene-by-Scene Breakdown

Scene 1 — Marketing (Demand Generation)
ABC Corporation's marketing team runs targeted campaigns to generate awareness among facility and operations managers. Oracle CX Sales tracks campaign responses and identifies potential leads.
Scene 2 — Lead (CX Sales)
A form-fill response comes in from J. Smith at XYZ Corporation. The lead is captured in Oracle CX Sales, reviewed and qualified by the inside sales team.
Scene 3 — Opportunity (CX Sales)
The qualified lead is converted into an Opportunity in Oracle CX Sales. The sales representative begins engaging with XYZ Corporation to understand their vending requirements.
Scene 4 — CPQ: Configure
The sales rep uses Oracle CPQ to configure the right vending solution — selecting the appropriate machines, service tiers and subscription options for XYZ Corporation.
Scene 5 — CPQ: Price and Approve
Oracle CPQ calculates the pricing based on the configuration. An automated approval workflow is triggered for deals above a threshold value. The manager approves the deal.
Scene 6 — CPQ: Quote
A formal quote is generated from Oracle CPQ. XYZ Corporation reviews and signs the Agreement of Commitment (AOC). The deal is officially accepted.
Scene 7 — Order Management: Orchestrate
Oracle Order Management receives the signed order from CPQ. It acts as the central orchestration hub — coordinating with Supply Chain, Field Service and Subscription Management so the entire order is fulfilled from a single point.
Scene 8 — Order Management: Sequence
The approved order is decomposed into its component line items and sequenced in the correct dependency order — the physical vending machines, the field-service installation and the intangible subscription service. Pricing, tax and shipping are frozen, and billing is deliberately held back until installation is confirmed so nothing is billed improperly.
Scene 9 — Subscription Management
Oracle Subscription Management activates the subscription for the ongoing vending service. It is created in Draft status carrying the frozen pricing from the order, and only moves to active billing once the field installation is confirmed.
Scene 10 — Accounts Receivable: Billing Schedule
With installation confirmed, Oracle Accounts Receivable generates the active billing schedule from the subscription and issues the first actual invoice to XYZ Corporation. J. Smith receives the invoice for the vending service.
Scene 11 — Accounts Receivable: Invoicing & Payment
The recurring billing cycle runs. Each period the actual invoice is generated against the billing schedule, XYZ Corporation pays, and the payment is applied — keeping the subscription revenue stream current.
Scene 12 — Revenue Management (RMCS)
Oracle Revenue Management (RMCS) takes over the accounting side. It allocates the contract value across performance obligations and recognises revenue over the subscription term in line with accounting standards — separating the recurring subscription revenue from one-time hardware and installation revenue.
Scene 13 — Fusion Service
After go-live, Oracle Fusion Service handles ongoing support. When XYZ Corporation logs a service request, entitlement and SLA coverage are verified against the subscription before a field technician is dispatched to service the vending machine.
Scene 14 — Renewal: Prepare
As the two-year contract nears its end — 180 days out — Renewal Management automatically prepares the renewal. A renewal quote is drafted from the existing subscription, and the rep builds a strategy that includes upsell (Basic to Premium) and cross-sell (adding office coffee service).
Scene 15 — Renewal: Upsell & Cross-Sell
The renewal is executed. The upsell and cross-sell flow back through CPQ and Order Management as an amendment and a new subscription line — closing the loop, and the whole Lead-to-Revenue cycle begins again for the next term.
