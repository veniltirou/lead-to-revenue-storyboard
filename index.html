About This Series

This storyboard series is designed for beginners and professionals who want to understand how Oracle CX products work together in a real business scenario — told through a simple, relatable story.

The Business Scenario

ABC Corporation is a specialty vending solutions provider. They have Oracle CX fully implemented across their entire business. ABC Corporation uses Oracle CX products and features to run their sales, service, order management, billing, and renewal operations efficiently from end to end.

XYZ Corporation is a networking company that needs vending solutions for their offices.

This series follows ABC Corporation as they identify, pursue, close, fulfill, and renew the business deal with XYZ Corporation — using Oracle CX every step of the way.

The Full Lead-to-Revenue Flow

Scene-by-Scene Breakdown

Scene 1 — Marketing (Demand Generation)
ABC Corporation's marketing team runs targeted campaigns to generate awareness among facility and operations managers. Oracle CX Sales is used to track campaign responses and identify potential leads.
Scene 2 — Lead (CX Sales)
A form-fill response comes in from J. Smith at XYZ Corporation. The lead is captured in Oracle CX Sales, reviewed and qualified by the inside sales team.
Scene 3 — Opportunity (CX Sales)
The qualified lead is converted into an Opportunity in Oracle CX Sales. The sales representative at ABC Corporation begins engaging with XYZ Corporation to understand their vending requirements.
Scene 4 — CPQ: Configure
The sales rep uses Oracle CPQ to configure the right vending solution for XYZ Corporation — selecting the appropriate machines, service tiers and subscription options.
Scene 5 — CPQ: Price and Approve
Oracle CPQ calculates the pricing based on the configuration. An automated approval workflow is triggered for deals above a threshold value. The manager approves the deal.
Scene 6 — CPQ: Quote
A formal quote is generated from Oracle CPQ. XYZ Corporation reviews and signs the Agreement of Commitment (AOC). The deal is officially accepted.
Scene 7 — Order Management: Orchestrate
Oracle Order Management receives the signed order from CPQ. It acts as the central orchestration hub — coordinating with Supply Chain, Field Service and Subscription Management simultaneously.
Scene 8 — Order Management: Sequence
The order is decomposed into line items — hardware (vending machines), field service installation and intangible subscription services. Pricing, tax and shipping are frozen. Dependencies are managed to prevent billing before installation is confirmed.
Scene 9 — Subscription Management
Once the installation is confirmed, Oracle Subscription Management activates the subscription for XYZ Corporation. The subscription starts in Draft status and moves to Active upon billing trigger.
Scene 10 & 11 — Accounts Receivable (AR)
Oracle Accounts Receivable generates the billing schedule and sends the actual invoice to XYZ Corporation. XYZ Corporation processes payment through their AP system.
Scene 12 — Revenue Management (RMCS)
Oracle Revenue Management recognizes and reports the total term revenue for the XYZ Corporation deal — ensuring strict revenue compliance and reporting accuracy.
Scene 13 — Fusion Service
XYZ Corporation raises a service request post-installation. Oracle Fusion Service verifies the entitlement — confirming the SLA coverage — and dispatches the appropriate support response.
Scene 14 — Renewal: Prepare
With 180 days remaining on the 2-year contract, ABC Corporation's renewal team prepares the renewal strategy. They identify upsell opportunities (premium tier) and cross-sell opportunities (office coffee service).
Scene 15 — Renewal: Upsell and Cross-Sell
The renewal loop is executed. ABC Corporation presents XYZ Corporation with an expanded offering — upselling to a premium vending tier and cross-selling an office coffee subscription — growing the account value.
