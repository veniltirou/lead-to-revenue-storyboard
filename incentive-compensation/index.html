Chapter 3

Oracle Incentive Compensation

A 13-scene storyboard series covering the complete Oracle Incentive Compensation engine — from compensation plan setup through commission calculation, payment processing and analytics — all through the ABC Corporation vending scenario.

Business Scenario: James, a Senior Sales Rep at ABC Corporation (Vending Solutions), closes a deal with XYZ Corporation (Networking Company) worth $78,500. This series follows how Oracle Incentive Compensation automatically calculates and pays James's commission — from the moment the deal closes to the payment appearing in his paycheck.

👥 Characters in This Series

James (GREEN shirt) — Senior Sales Rep at ABC Corporation who closes the XYZ Corporation deal
Sarah (NAVY blazer) — Sales Manager at ABC Corporation who supports and oversees James
Lisa (ORANGE blazer) — Compensation Admin who configures and manages Oracle IC at ABC Corporation
Tom (WHITE shirt + TIE) — New Admin learning Oracle IC from Lisa

Scene 1 — James Wins the XYZ Corporation Deal

The Business Context — How Oracle IC Gets Triggered

📱 On mobile? Pinch to zoom to read the details clearly.

What this shows: James closes the XYZ Corporation deal — 50 vending machines, 36-month subscription, $78,500 total contract value. The moment the deal is marked Closed Won in Oracle CX Sales, Oracle Incentive Compensation automatically picks it up and begins the commission calculation process. Sarah confirms that no manual intervention is needed — Oracle IC handles everything automatically.

Scene 2 — Compensation Plan Setup

How ABC Corporation Defines What James Earns

📱 On mobile? Pinch to zoom to read the details clearly.

What this shows: Lisa, the Compensation Admin, assigns James to the Senior Sales Rep — Vending 2026 Compensation Plan. The plan has two components — Component 1 is a transaction-based Vending Machine Sales Commission that pays on every deal closed, and Component 2 is an objective-based Quarterly Territory Bonus that pays when James hits strategic targets. A Compensation Plan defines HOW a sales rep earns money — it contains plan components, performance measures, incentive formulas and effective dates.

Scene 3 — Performance Measures and Incentive Formulas

The Scoreboard and the Calculation Rule

📱 On mobile? Pinch to zoom to read the details clearly.

What this shows: Lisa explains to Tom the two core building blocks of Oracle IC. The Performance Measure is the scoreboard — it tracks what James sold (Revenue Booked — Vending, measured monthly, James's YTD total is $78,500). The Incentive Formula is the calculation rule — it takes James's score from the Performance Measure and converts it into a dollar amount using a Tiered Commission Formula that looks up the Rate Table based on revenue tier. They always work together: Measure tracks → Formula calculates → Earning generated.

Scene 4 — Rate Tables

Exactly What Percentage James Earns at Each Revenue Level

📱 On mobile? Pinch to zoom to read the details clearly.

What this shows: The Senior Sales Rep Vending Commission Rate Table for 2026. Tier 1 — $0 to $50,000 earns 5%. Tier 2 — $50,001 to $100,000 earns 7%. Tier 3 — $100,001 to $150,000 earns 9%. Tier 4 — above $150,000 earns 12%. Applied to James's XYZ Corporation deal of $78,500 — the first $50,000 earns 5% = $2,500, the next $28,500 earns 7% = $1,995, giving a total commission of $4,495. Rate Tables reward higher performance — the more James sells, the higher his commission rate.

Scene 5 — Participants, Roles and Pay Groups

Registering James in Oracle IC Before Calculations Begin

📱 On mobile? Pinch to zoom to read the details clearly.

What this shows: Three setup steps required before Oracle IC can process any earnings for James. Participant Record — James Mitchell, Employee ID EMP-00445, Senior Sales Rep, Active status. Role Assignment — Senior Sales Rep role, Northern California territory, Vending Machines product line, assigned to Senior Sales Rep 2026 plan. Pay Group — Monthly Commission Field Sales group, monthly payment frequency, USD currency. All three must be correctly configured or Oracle IC cannot calculate or pay James's commission.

Scene 6 — Automated Role Assignment and Classification Rules

How Oracle IC Assigns Plans Automatically — No Manual Work

📱 On mobile? Pinch to zoom to read the details clearly.

What this shows: Lisa explains to Tom that Classification Rules eliminate manual plan assignment. Rule 1 — if Job Title is Senior Sales Rep and Territory is Northern California, then assign Senior Sales Rep 2026 plan. Rule 2 — if Job Title is Sales Rep, assign Sales Rep Standard 2026. Rule 3 — if Job Title is Sales Manager, assign Manager Overlay 2026. When HR promotes James to Senior Sales Rep, the Classification Rule fires instantly and Oracle IC automatically moves him to the correct plan. No manual work. Full audit trail of every assignment change.

Scene 7 — Sales Crediting and Credit Categories

Who Gets Credit for the XYZ Corporation Deal?

📱 On mobile? Pinch to zoom to read the details clearly.

What this shows: Sarah asks Lisa how credit is distributed since she also supported the XYZ deal. Oracle IC assigns three types of credit — James Mitchell receives 100% Direct Credit as the primary rep who owns and closes the deal ($78,500). Sarah Johnson receives 100% Overlay Credit as the supporting manager ($78,500) — importantly this does NOT reduce James's credit, both earn independently. Mike Chen receives 50% Split Credit as the pre-sales support rep ($39,250). Each person's commission is calculated based on their credited amount independently.

Scene 8 — Sales Crediting Rules

The Rules Engine That Distributes Credit Automatically

📱 On mobile? Pinch to zoom to read the details clearly.

What this shows: The four Sales Crediting Rules configured at ABC Corporation. Rule 1 — Primary Rep Direct Credit: if Opportunity Owner is a Sales Rep, assign 100% Direct Credit to that rep. Rule 2 — Manager Overlay Credit: if the Sales Rep has an assigned Manager, assign 100% Overlay Credit to the Manager. Rule 3 — Pre-Sales Support Split: if a Pre-Sales Rep is tagged on the Opportunity, assign 50% Split Credit to them. Rule 4 — Territory Override: if the deal territory differs from the rep's primary territory, flag for manual review. These rules ensure the right people get credit automatically for every deal with full auditability.

Scene 9 — Transactions and Earning Transactions

From Closed Deal to Commission Dollar Amount

📱 On mobile? Pinch to zoom to read the details clearly.

What this shows: The XYZ Corporation deal is imported into Oracle IC as Transaction TXN-2026-004521 — source Oracle CX Sales, amount $78,500, participant James Mitchell, status Processed. Oracle IC then processes it and generates Earning Transaction EARN-2026-004521 — applying the Senior Sales Rep 2026 plan, calculating Tier 1 ($50,000 × 5% = $2,500) and Tier 2 ($28,500 × 7% = $1,995) giving Total Earning $4,495, status Calculated. Transaction is the input — the raw deal data. Earning Transaction is the output — the calculated commission amount.

Scene 10 — The Calculation Process

6 Automated Steps From Transaction to Net Payable Amount

📱 On mobile? Pinch to zoom to read the details clearly.

What this shows: Lisa runs the monthly calculation batch which processes all 47 transactions for all participants in one automated run. Step 1 — Import Transactions from Oracle CX Sales. Step 2 — Apply Sales Crediting Rules to assign credit to James, Sarah and Mike. Step 3 — Update Performance Measures, updating James's YTD Revenue to $78,500. Step 4 — Apply Incentive Formula using the Tiered Rate Table, result $4,495. Step 5 — Generate Earning Transaction EARN-2026-004521. Step 6 — Hold and Recovery Check, net payable $4,495 with no deductions. The entire 6-step process runs automatically — Lisa triggers it once.

Scene 11 — Objectives Based Performance Incentive Plan

James's Q2 2026 Quarterly Bonus — Beyond Transaction Commission

📱 On mobile? Pinch to zoom to read the details clearly.

What this shows: In addition to his transaction-based commission, James also has a Quarterly Objectives Based Incentive Plan with a maximum bonus of $3,000. Objective 1 — Quarterly Revenue Target $150,000, current attainment 52% ($78,500), bonus earned so far $735. Objective 2 — New Customer Acquisition target 3, current 1 (XYZ Corporation), attainment 33%, earned $333. Objective 3 — Pipeline Coverage Ratio target 3x, current 47%, earned $235. Total bonus earned to date $1,303. James runs both plans simultaneously in Oracle IC — transaction commission AND quarterly bonus.

Scene 12 — Payment Plan and Payment Processing

James Gets Paid — From Oracle IC to Payroll

📱 On mobile? Pinch to zoom to read the details clearly.

What this shows: The Payment Plan for James — Monthly Commission Field Sales pay group, monthly frequency, USD currency, 0% hold, 90-day clawback on deal cancellation. Lisa runs the May 2026 Payment Processing batch — Vending Machine Commission $4,495, Q2 Quarterly Bonus partial $1,303, Hold Deduction $0, Recovery Deduction $0, Net Payment to Payroll $5,798. Status sent to Oracle Fusion HCM Payroll, expected in James's June 1, 2026 paycheck. Oracle IC integrates directly with Oracle Fusion HCM Payroll — no manual data entry between systems.

Scene 13 — Reports and Analytics

Full Visibility for Managers and Sales Reps

What this shows: Two dashboards running simultaneously. Sarah's Manager Dashboard shows team performance — Team Revenue $373,500, Team Commission $25,890, Average Attainment 62%, with individual rep breakdowns showing James at 52%, Amanda Torres at 75%, Kevin Park at 97% and Priya Nair at 25%. Transparency builds trust — when James can verify every calculation, disputes are eliminated and motivation stays high.
